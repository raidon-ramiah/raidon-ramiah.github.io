Neuroplasticity

The Brain's Map

Learning can physically change your brain. Neural connections can become stronger the more you learn. A neural connection is a literally a connection between neurons or groups of neurons. The brain is continuously reinforcing connection circuits to make them more efficient and accesible, while lesser used circuits are pruned off. Brains are built over time, its not like Einstein was born knowing physics. So what does this mean for learning? It means that, the more you practice and learn about something, over time your brain will adapt to this. It will create more neuron connections, and become more efficient at firing chains of these processes. The connections physically become stronger and denser over time. Effective learning allows you to change your brain. Understanding this actually sets you up to be a more capable and willing learner. When people know that their brains are actually dynamic systems that change over time they can adopt a growth mindset.

A growth mindset is a mindset where everything becomes an opportunity to grow and learn. When setbacks occur, people with a fixed mindset would typically think that they are not capable of overcoming it, and that it is beyond their capabilities. The same setback can happen to someone with a growth mindset, the difference is that someone with a growth mindset recognises that challenges also present an opportunity to learn. Challanges can (not always) pull weaknesses and insufficiencies to the forefront of everything. The beauty of a growth mindset is that when you are confronted with challenges, whether they are everyday problems or major ones, is that it allows you to improve yourself so that you can manage more things over time. Whether it is a weakness of yours, or achieving a deeper understanding of something you already know, a growth mindset is one that understands that our brains our dynamic and are always changing. A fixed mindset simply accepts its inabilities as permanent traits that cannot be changed. Which, if you think that, they will not.
