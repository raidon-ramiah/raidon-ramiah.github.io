Problem Solving

The time...

  • I was stuck on a simple problem
  • I couldn't count backwards from the highest number to the lowest number. Despite it being seemingly a simple challenge, I couldn't seem to solve it. I double and triple-checked my logic, but I could not work out what the issue was. I asked for help in the tech-questions channel after this. I got help, firstly through understanding arrays and indexes better, then also understanding what array.length does. After learning more about arrays and indexes, I was able to solve my problem.
  • that I elegantly solved a problem
  • After learning more about forEach loops and object methods I was able to do some cool things on the katas. I learnt about them after being stuck on previous problems involving them. When I had to do a new kata I was able to use my prior knowledge to complete most of it, and used a bit of google and MDN Web Docs to fill in the details. I was able to solve the rest of the katas fairly easily using more efficient, more straightforward methods. It felt great doing this, and I learnt that sometimes a setback can lead to greater success if you learn from it.
  • that I was reluctant to ask for help

I spent ages stuck on a bunch of them and my stubbornness ended up hindering my progress. The next week, if I was stuck I just asked for help and it kept me moving forward productively.
