About Me // The Who

When deciding to join Dev Academy, I had a number of things of things on my mind. I had to make a decision for my future versus my present. The idea of doing this course had to be weighed against the time value of just working an entry-level job. Now that I'm studying here, I'm glad I made the right choice.

My culture is one that is all about acceptance, learning, and connections. These are just a few things from a long list of things I've learnt from my family and those around me.

My strengths are my logical and creative thinking. I think that I have a strong sense of both logic and out-of-the-box thinking that allows me to approach challenges from differing angles and ultimately learn from these experiences.

Unfortunately, my thinking style can also be a weakness as I sometimes forget to do the simple things. My career devleopment in this industry is non-existent and I just hope to achieve as much as I can so that I may be able to change that.

When working on a group film project in high school, someone else and I had different ideas about how it should be made. My friend wanted to make this a comedy and I wanted to make a horror. Keep in mind that the two are surprisingly similar (as shown here ). Ultimately, we couldn't compromise or it would have been the worst of both worlds. I decided to go with my friends idea because I did bleieve in it, and put my all into making it as good as possinle. The result was a project I was really happy with, despite it not being what I had originally envisioned. At the end of the day, communication is always the most important thing in a group dynamic.
