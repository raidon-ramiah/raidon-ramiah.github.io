Final Reflections

  • What are three new things I've learnt about myself and my ego during the core learning?
  • 1. The first thing I learnt was the importance of reflection. Reflection allowed me to learn from my experiences, even if there were no egregious mistakes. This really made me more thoughtful and deliberate in my problem solving and learning.
  • 2. I found the neuroplasticity part of core very intriguing. I've begun learning with an underlying belief that I am remoulding my brain every time I practice effectively.
  • 3. I learnt that sometimes its okay to ask for help. I always find that the frustration from a simple mistake is a good learning point to ensure that it never happens sgain, however sometimes I was so stuck that I needed to ask for help, and that was okay. Programming is hard. The assistance is there for me if I ask, and often I learn so much more when I do, so for me learning when to ask for help was impactful.
  • What are the roles of values, empathy, and self awareness in learning and programing?
  • Your values are everything, they shape who you are, how you act, and why you act as such. Our entire learning journey, which ideally includes our future as developers, is therefore just a consequence of underlying values and goals. Empathy and self awareness are key to working within a team, or working with people that come from different backgrounds. Everyone has different values, goals, and backgrounds. Being aware of that, and being self aware to know how you fit in to this medley is half the puzzle. The other half is empathy. Empathy is actually acknowledging everyone around you and being caring.
  • What has surprised you the most about core learning?
  • Why do you think we are spending so much time focusing on core learning?
  • Do you think the time you spent studying core learning felt like a waste of time?
  • Honestly, I initially thought core learning going to be about learning platitudes, and that core learning would be less of a focus. I was completely wrong, core learning teaches very important lessons and skills that are incredibly important as a learner. I think Dev Academy has a focus on core because of all the beenfits of core learning, and its applicability and importance in the real world. In reality, people will only want to work with someone who they want to work with. What I mean by this is that someone who is reflective, communicative, empathetic, and self aware is a superior programmer and colleague. That is why I do not think the time I spent learning core was a waste of time.
  • What are the most challenging aspects of the core learning?
  • The most difficult aspects were probably when I had to reflect on myself and my background. I usually tend to think about the future rather than the past, so it was a bit of change to reflect on everything, but I am glad I did because it is so incrdibly useful.
